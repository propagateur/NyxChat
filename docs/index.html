Built for privacy, not for tracking

Every part of NyxChat is designed to keep your conversations between you and your contacts.

🔒

End-to-end encrypted

Each message and file is sealed for its recipient with X25519 key exchange and XSalsa20-Poly1305. Verify a contact by comparing fingerprints.

🛰️

No server, ever

There is no account and no backend to trust or to breach. Your device owns your identity and talks directly to your peers.

🧅

Reachable over Tor

Each device publishes a Tor onion service, so friends can reach you from anywhere without opening ports or sharing your IP.

📡

Automatic local discovery

On the same network, peers find each other automatically through libp2p and mDNS — no setup, no codes to exchange.

🎙️

Calls & voice messages

Encrypted audio and video calls with device selection, plus voice messages and drag-and-drop encrypted file transfer.

🌍

Nine languages

English, French, Spanish, German, Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Polish and Russian — switch instantly in settings.

How it works

Three steps, no accounts, no waiting on a server.

1

Get your identity

On first launch your device creates a local key pair and a Tor address. Nothing is registered anywhere.

2

Add a contact

Peers on your network appear on their own. For remote friends, share your onion address or paste theirs.

3

Talk securely

Keys are exchanged directly, then every message, file and call is encrypted end to end between you.

Your data stays on your device

By default NyxChat keeps no conversation history on disk — messages disappear when you close the app. Network traffic is always encrypted, local discovery uses libp2p, and Internet reachability uses Tor onion services. It is built with Tauri, Rust, React, libp2p, Tor and WebRTC.

NyxChat uses established cryptographic primitives but has not had a formal third-party audit. Treat it as an experimental private messenger, not as protection for high-risk secrets.

macOS: "NyxChat is damaged and can't be opened"?

The build isn't signed with an Apple Developer ID, so macOS quarantines it on download. After moving NyxChat to Applications, run this once in Terminal, then open it normally: 

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/NyxChat.app
