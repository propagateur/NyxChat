Wait — what is “Chat Control”?

It's a proposed EU regulation that would push apps to scan your private messages on your own device — yes, including encrypted ones — and report what an algorithm decides looks suspicious. It's marketed as safety. Cryptographers have a shorter word for scanning everyone's messages all the time: surveillance.

Hundreds of security researchers signed open letters explaining that you cannot scan encrypted messages without breaking the encryption for everyone. A backdoor doesn't check ID at the door.

NyxChat's answer isn't a promise — it's the architecture. There's no company server to hand a scanning order to, and your keys never leave your device. We couldn't read your messages if a regulator asked us nicely. Or not so nicely.

How it works, in one breath

Open the app and it makes you a key pair and a Tor address — no signup. People on your Wi-Fi show up on their own; for everyone else, swap onion addresses once. Then every message, file and call is sealed end to end with X25519 and XSalsa20-Poly1305. That's it.
